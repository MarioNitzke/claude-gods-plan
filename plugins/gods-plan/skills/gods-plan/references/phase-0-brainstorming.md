# Phase 0: Brainstorming

Turn a rough idea into a clear direction through conversation. Before anyone writes code or even a plan, make sure you understand what you're building and why.

## Don't skip this, even for "simple" things

Every project goes through brainstorming. A todo app, a one-function utility, a config tweak — all of them. "Simple" projects are exactly where unexamined assumptions waste the most time. The output can be short (a few sentences for genuinely tiny things), but you still present it and get approval.

**Hard gate:** Don't move to Phase 1 until the user has approved a design direction. No exceptions.

## How it works

**1. Get the lay of the land**
- Check files, docs, recent commits to understand where things stand
- Look for existing patterns, similar features, relevant conventions

**2. Ask questions (2-4 at a time)**
- Don't drag it out one question at a time — batch them
- Multiple choice when possible, easier for everyone
- Focus on: what's the purpose, what are the constraints, how do we know it's done
- Keep asking until the picture is clear

**3. Propose 2-3 approaches**
- Each with trade-offs, not just pros
- Lead with your recommendation and explain why
- Keep it conversational

**4. Present the direction and get a thumbs-up**
- Scale detail to complexity: a few sentences for simple, up to 200-300 words for nuanced parts
- Check in: "Does this direction work?"
- Cover: architecture, key components, data flow, testing approach
- Be ready to circle back if something's off

## Principles
- Cut ruthlessly (YAGNI) — strip out anything that isn't truly needed
- Show alternatives before committing
- Stay flexible — if something doesn't land, adjust
